    Since June of this year, I've been attempting to learn web development through books and online resources. I'v read Apprenticeship Patterns, Whyâ€™s (Poignant) Guide to Ruby, Learn Python the Hard Way, and many other books. I've tried codeschool, codeacademy, treehouse, codewars and an assortment of other sites posting tutorials. I've signed up for email newsletters, added blogs to my rss reader, and listened to at least a dozen different podcast. And after all that, I still feel like a beginner.

    When I started this, I was thinking of picking up a few skills to add to my resume. Another way to make myself more employable. As a college dropout, I didn't see many options for myself and I couldn't bring myself to go back to school without a clear plan. The past few years before then left such a bad taste in my mouth that I was striving for a complete do over. So many of my peers were talking about their passion and I didn't feel like I had one.

    I went through several rabbit holes and I found myself doing the web design coruse track at treehouse. Once coding came into the picture, I was hooked. At first, I didn't know what I found so appealing about it. I just remember thinking about how amazing it was to create a website. Following through from design to user interactions to user authentication. Half of things I saw didn't make sense but I took a little bit of what did and stored it in my brain. I went through multiple course tracks trying to absorb as much information as I can.

    The past few months have been like solving a jigsaw puzzle. I take the pieces I find along the way and try to piece it together. Sometimes I can see the bigger picture and sometimes I don't but the pieces themselves feel like precious gemstones waiting to be cut, polished and presented someday.

    This blog will be about that. I'll try to make sense of what I found and pick up more things along the way. Also, after further thinking, I've decided to ammend that first paragraph. I'm an advanced beginner trying to get to intermediate.
